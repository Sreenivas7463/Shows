Day 1: Historical Landmarks & Gardens

Morning Itinerary

Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace

Explore this 18th-century palace, a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture, and its museum.

Bangalore Fort (Kempegowda's Fort)

Walk to this 16th-century fort, built by Bangalore's founder.

KR Market (Krishnarajendra City Market)

Head to this bustling local market for shopping, photography, and to experience the local atmosphere.

Lal Bagh Botanical Garden

Visit this 240-acre botanical garden, a living museum of rare plants, featuring the iconic Glass House.

Food Recommendations

Breakfast/Lunch

Enjoy traditional South Indian breakfast or lunch at MTR (Mavalli Tiffin Room) or Vidyarthi Bhavan, famous for dosas, idlis, and filter coffee. You can also find South Indian food like idli or dosa at KR Market.

Afternoon Itinerary

Bangalore Palace

Visit this majestic structure built in 1878, modeled after England's Windsor Castle.

Vidhana Soudha

Admire the impressive seat of Karnataka's state legislature (exterior view, as interior access is usually restricted).

Cubbon Park

Spend a relaxing time in this sprawling green space, offering walking paths and botanical assets.

Bull Temple (Nandi Temple)

Visit this 16th-century temple known for its massive monolithic statue of Nandi.

Evening Itinerary

Food Street in VV Puram

Indulge in local street food like chaats, dosas, and kebabs.

Shopping & Nightlife (MG Road, Brigade Road, Commercial Street)

Explore popular streets like MG Road, Brigade Road, or Commercial Street for shopping, eating, and soaking in the city vibes.

UB City

Visit this luxury shopping and dining complex for a drink at a rooftop bar or a fine dining experience.

Day 2: Culture, Nature & Entertainment

Morning Itinerary

ISKCON Temple

Begin your day with a visit to this prominent temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, known for its stunning architecture and serene atmosphere.

Vidhana Soudha

(If not fully explored on Day 1) Admire its architectural brilliance (exterior view).

Food Recommendations

Lunch

Consider Vidyarthi Bhavan for crispy dosas and authentic South Indian cuisine. The Higher Taste inside ISKCON Temple offers Sattvic vegetarian food (without onion and garlic).

Midday/Afternoon Itinerary

Cubbon Park

Spend relaxing moments in this lush green space. Consider a toy train ride or a visit to the Bangalore Aquarium within the park.

Bannerghatta National Park

Visit this impressive wildlife habitat with a safari, butterfly park, and museum.

Evening Itinerary

Shopping & Dining (MG Road, Brigade Road, Church Street)

Explore MG Road, Brigade Road, and Church Street for shopping, dining, and Bangalore's nightlife.

UB City Mall

For luxury shopping or fine dining.

Dinner at Toit Brewpub

Unwind at Toit Brewpub for craft beers and delicious food like wood-fired pizzas or fish and chips.

Day 3: Art, History & Local Immersion (or Adventure)

Morning Itinerary

Cubbon Park

Start your day with a relaxing stroll in this sprawling green space.

National Gallery of Modern Art

Head to this gallery to explore a collection of contemporary Indian art.

Food Recommendations

Lunch

Have lunch at Brahmin's Coffee Bar, known for its filter coffee and idlis.

Afternoon Itinerary

Bangalore Fort

Revisit or explore this historic fort dating back to the 16th century.

Commercial Street

Conclude your day with a visit to this bustling shopping destination for clothes, jewelry, and souvenirs.

Evening Itinerary

Koramangala District

Explore Koramangala district for its trendy cafes, restaurants, and nightlife to conclude your Bangalore adventure.

Alternative Day 3 - Adventure & Spirituality

Morning: Nandi Hills

Consider an early drive to Nandi Hills to witness the sunrise and hike to the hilltop fort.

Afternoon/Evening: ISKCON Temple

After returning to the city, visit the ISKCON Temple for a spiritual experience.

Other Notable Places for Day 3 Consideration (if time permits)

  • Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum: Offers interactive exhibits on science and technology.
  • Ulsoor Lake: A serene spot for a boat ride or relaxation.
  • Bull Temple: (If not visited on Day 1) This historic temple is famous for its massive granite bull statue.
  • HAL Heritage Centre and Aerospace Museum: An engaging and educational destination to explore the evolution of aviation in India.
