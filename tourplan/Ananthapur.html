Anantapur: A Comprehensive 3-Day Tour Guide

Explore the spiritual, architectural, and natural wonders of Anantapur.

Day 1 Day 2 Day 3

Day 1: Spiritual Journey to Puttaparthi & Anantapur City Landmarks

Daily Itinerary

  • Morning: Depart for Prasanthi Nilayam (Puttaparthi) (approx. 80 km from Anantapur). Explore the spiritual center, attend prayer sessions (Aumkar, Veda Chanting, Bhajans), and have Darshan of Bhagawan's MahaSamadhi.
  • Lunch: Enjoy a vegetarian meal at one of the Canteens within Prasanthi Nilayam (South Indian, North Indian, or Western Canteen) or a local eatery in Puttaparthi.
  • Afternoon: Drive back to Anantapur and visit the ISKCON Temple (Radha Parthasarathi Temple), known for its distinct chariot-like design.
  • Evening: Explore the heart of Anantapur city, visiting the iconic Anantapur Clock Tower, a historical landmark commemorating India's Independence.

Specific Attractions

Prasanthi Nilayam (Puttaparthi)

ISKCON Temple (Radha Parthasarathi Temple)

Anantapur Clock Tower

Food Recommendations

  • Breakfast: Light South Indian breakfast (e.g., Idli, Dosa) before departure.
  • Lunch: Authentic vegetarian meal at a Prasanthi Nilayam Canteen.
  • Dinner: Savor Biryani at Hyderabad Chefs Restaurant or SS Paradise Restaurant. For pure vegetarian options, Hotel Vanta Gadhi is recommended.

Day 2: Vijayanagara Architectural Marvels

Daily Itinerary

  • Morning: Depart for Lepakshi Temple (Veerabhadra Temple) (approx. 120 km from Anantapur). Spend the morning marveling at this 16th-century masterpiece of Vijayanagara architecture, renowned for its intricate carvings, stunning frescoes, the large Nandi statue, and the iconic "hanging pillar."
  • Lunch: Have lunch at a local restaurant in Lepakshi or a suitable eatery on the way to Penukonda.
  • Afternoon: Proceed to Penukonda Fort (approx. 70 km from Anantapur). Explore this majestic structure built by the Vijayanagara kings, including the Gagan Mahal and Babayya Darga.
  • Evening: Drive back to Anantapur.

Specific Attractions

Lepakshi Temple (Veerabhadra Temple)

Penukonda Fort

Food Recommendations

  • Breakfast: Try Pesarattu with ginger chutney for a traditional start.
  • Lunch: Opt for a simple, authentic Andhra meal at a local dhabha or a small restaurant en route.
  • Dinner: Sample Gutti Vankaya Curry or an Andhra Thali at Sri Roopa Multicuisine Restaurant for a comprehensive local experience.

Day 3: Nature's Grandeur & Ancient Shrines

Daily Itinerary

  • Morning: Depart for Thimmamma Marrimanu (approx. 107 km from Anantapur), one of the largest banyan trees in South India, covering 5 acres. Visit the quaint temple beneath it.
  • Lunch: Enjoy a meal at a restaurant near the attraction or a highway restaurant like Blue Moon Highway Restaurant on NH7.
  • Afternoon: Head to Bugga Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple near Tadipatri (approx. 55 km east of Anantapur). Discover this temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, famous for its natural underground water spring trickling onto the Lingam. (Optional: If time and energy permit, visit Guguddu (Pir Kullayappa Shrine), 29 km from Anantapur, for a unique pluralistic devotional experience.)
  • Evening: Drive back to Anantapur.

Specific Attractions

Thimmamma Marrimanu

Bugga Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple

Guguddu (Pir Kullayappa Shrine)

Food Recommendations

  • Breakfast: Enjoy Idli and Dosa from a local favorite like Gurukrupa Hotel or Sujatha Hotel.
  • Lunch: A hearty meal at Blue Moon Highway Restaurant.
  • Dinner: Indulge in local snacks like Punugulu or traditional sweets like Rava Kesari or Bobbattu, followed by dinner at a restaurant of your choice. Many eateries in Anantapur offer 24-hour service.
